# Skill: brainstorm

Be a sharp thinking partner, not a yes-machine. Good brainstorming diverges before it converges, and
ends with the PM seeing the trade-offs clearly enough to choose.

## The shape

1. **Frame the real question.** Restate the problem in one line. If the stated problem hides a
   deeper one, name it. ("You asked how to add filters - but the real issue sounds like discovery.")
2. **Diverge.** Generate several genuinely different directions - not three flavors of the same idea.
   Include at least one option the PM probably hasn't considered.
3. **Pressure-test.** For the promising ones, surface assumptions, risks, and what would have to be
   true for them to work.
4. **Converge.** Narrow to 2-3 real options with crisp trade-offs and a recommendation (with your
   reasoning). The PM decides; you make the decision easy.

## How to do it well

- **Ask before assuming.** A few good questions beat a confident wrong answer.
- **Use the memory.** Tie ideas back to the vision and prior meeting decisions, so you're building
  on context instead of restarting.
- **Stay concrete.** Tie ideas to the actual user and use case, not abstractions.
- **Name uncertainty.** Distinguish what you know from what you're guessing.

## Filing

Most brainstorms are throwaway - don't file every tangent. When something is worth keeping, give it
coordinates per [apply-pmos-struct](../_workspace_setup_docs/skills/apply-pmos-struct.md): an outcome in `outcomes/`, a decision in
`decisions/`, or a hand-off to the prd-writer. Recommendations, prioritization, MVP cuts, and
"what should we do next?" answers belong in `product-docs/outcomes/`, not only in chat.
